# Hook Library

> 90+ proven hook templates organized by psychological trigger. Each uses [bracketed placeholders] for universal adaptation.

---

## How to Use This Library

1. **Choose your framework** from `viral-frameworks.md`
2. **Select a hook category** that matches your content's emotional angle
3. **Pick a template** and fill in the brackets
4. **Test the "scroll stop" rule:** Would YOU stop scrolling to read this? If not, pick a different hook.

### Hook Quality Checklist
- [ ] Creates curiosity, tension, or an emotional reaction in under 2 seconds
- [ ] Works as a standalone sentence (doesn't need context)
- [ ] Is specific enough to feel credible (numbers, details)
- [ ] Doesn't give away the full answer (keeps them reading)
- [ ] Is one sentence, max two short sentences

## Category 1: Curiosity Gap

The brain cannot resist an open loop. These hooks create a gap between what the reader knows and what they want to know.

### Templates

1. "[Number] [professionals] do this wrong. Here's the fix."
2. "I [achieved outcome] in [timeframe]. Not by [expected method]."
3. "Nobody talks about the [hidden aspect] of [popular topic]."
4. "The [industry] secret that [authority figures] won't share."
5. "What [big number] [people] know that you don't."
6. "I studied [number] [things] to find the [number] that actually [outcome]."
7. "There's a reason [successful person/company] does [unexpected thing]."
8. "The real reason your [thing] isn't [desired outcome]."
9. "I asked [number] [experts] for their #1 [topic] tip. One answer shocked me."
10. "Everything you know about [topic] is based on a [myth/mistake/lie]."
11. "[Famous company] is quietly doing [thing] and nobody is paying attention."
12. "The [topic] mistake I see [frequency] that takes [time] to fix."
13. "Why [successful people] never [common practice] (and what they do instead)."
14. "I [action] every day for [timeframe]. Here's what nobody warned me about."
15. "There's a [number]-second trick that [outcome]. Most [people] will never try it."

### Filled Examples

**Tech:**
- "90% of developers do code reviews wrong. Here's the fix."
- "I shipped a product in 14 days. Not by writing more code."
- "Nobody talks about the loneliness of being a senior engineer."

**Career:**
- "I landed 3 job offers in 2 weeks. Not by applying online."
- "The hiring secret that recruiters won't share with candidates."
- "I asked 50 CEOs for their #1 career tip. One answer shocked me."

**Entrepreneurship:**
- "I studied 200 failed startups to find the 3 patterns that killed them."
- "There's a reason Stripe does customer support differently and nobody is paying attention."
- "The pricing mistake I see weekly that takes 6 months to recover from."

## Category 2: Bold Claim

These hooks make a declarative statement that challenges the status quo. They work because the reader immediately agrees or disagrees -- either way, they keep reading.

### Templates

1. "[Common practice] is dead. Here's what replaced it."
2. "You don't need [popular thing] to [desired outcome]."
3. "Stop [common activity]. Start [better alternative]."
4. "[Popular advice] is the worst advice in [industry]."
5. "I deleted [popular tool] and my [metric] went up [X]%."
6. "[Industry/role] doesn't have a [common problem] problem. It has a [real problem] problem."
7. "The [thing everyone wants] is actually the [thing that hurts you]."
8. "[Year] is the last year [current practice] will work."
9. "You're not [negative state]. You're just [reframe]."
10. "The best [professionals] I know don't [expected behavior]."
11. "[Prestigious thing] is the most overrated [category] in [industry]."
12. "Your [thing] is already good enough. Your [other thing] is what's broken."
13. "[Number]% of [people] are wasting time on [activity]. Here's proof."
14. "If you're still [common practice] in [year], you're already behind."
15. "The [thing] that got you [past achievement] will prevent you from [next achievement]."

### Filled Examples

**Tech:**
- "Unit tests are dead. Here's what replaced them."
- "You don't need a CS degree to become a senior engineer."
- "I deleted Jira and my team's velocity went up 40%."

**Career:**
- "Networking events are the worst career advice in existence."
- "Stop perfecting your resume. Start building your reputation."
- "The best leaders I know don't have an open-door policy."

**Entrepreneurship:**
- "Raising VC funding is the most overrated milestone in startups."
- "Your product is already good enough. Your distribution is what's broken."
- "If you're still doing cold outreach in 2026, you're already behind."

## Category 3: Number Drop

Numbers create instant credibility and specificity. The contrast between large and small numbers creates tension that demands explanation.

### Templates

1. "[Big number] [metric]. [Small number] [unexpected thing]. Here's why."
2. "I spent [time] studying [topic]. Here are [N] truths nobody mentions."
3. "[N] years. [N] projects. [N] [outcomes]. One lesson:"
4. "[Big number] people tried [thing]. Only [small number] did [this]."
5. "It took me [big number] [attempts] to learn this [small number]-step process."
6. "[Percentage]% of [group] fail at [thing]. The [small percentage]% who succeed all do [this]."
7. "I've [action] [big number] times. Here are the [small number] [things] that actually matter."
8. "In [timeframe], I went from [small number] to [big number] [metric]. Here's the breakdown."
9. "[Big number] [hours/dollars/attempts]. [Small number] [results]. What went wrong."
10. "I analyzed [number] [things] and found [number] patterns nobody expected."
11. "[Number] [time units] ago, I [starting state]. Today: [impressive current state]."
12. "My [thing] has [impressive number]. My secret? Just [small number] [actions]."
13. "[Number]-figure [outcome] from a [humble number]-[thing] [starting point]."
14. "After [number] [experiences], I can tell you: [thing] comes down to [small number] [factors]."
15. "[Small number] [changes]. [Big number] [results]. The math doesn't make sense until you try it."

### Filled Examples

**Tech:**
- "100,000 lines of code. 3 that caused the outage. Here's why."
- "I spent 2,000 hours studying system design. Here are 5 truths nobody mentions."
- "12 years. 47 projects. 200+ deployments. One lesson:"

**Career:**
- "I applied to 300 jobs. Got 3 interviews. Then changed one thing and got 15."
- "87% of managers fail at giving feedback. The 13% who succeed all do this."
- "After 500 interviews, I can tell you: hiring comes down to 2 factors."

**Entrepreneurship:**
- "6-figure revenue from a 500-subscriber newsletter. The math doesn't make sense until you try it."
- "$0 to $1M ARR. 18 months. 0 paid ads. Here's the breakdown."
- "I analyzed 1,000 landing pages and found 4 patterns nobody expected."

## Category 4: Story Opener

These hooks drop the reader into a specific moment. The vividness and specificity signal that a real story follows, which activates the brain's narrative-processing mode (highest dwell time).

### Templates

1. "3 years ago, I was [relatable low point]."
2. "My [person/boss/client] said something that changed everything."
3. "I almost [quit/failed/gave up] on [day/date]."
4. "The email that changed my [career/life/business]:"
5. "Nobody warned me about [unexpected challenge]."
6. "It was [specific day/time] when I realized [pivotal insight]."
7. "The worst [professional moment] of my life happened on a [ordinary day detail]."
8. "Someone told me '[specific quote]' and I haven't been the same since."
9. "I was sitting in [specific place] when I got the [call/message/news]."
10. "They said no. [Number] times. Then I [what changed]."
11. "The day I [dramatic action] was the day everything got better."
12. "I'll never forget the moment [person] looked at me and said:"
13. "[Specific time]. [Specific place]. I made the decision that [outcome]."
14. "Last [day/week/month], something happened that I need to share."
15. "I wasn't supposed to be in the room. But I was. And I heard [thing]."
16. "The conversation lasted [short time]. It changed the next [long time] of my [career/life]."

### Filled Examples

**Tech:**
- "3 years ago, I was writing code 14 hours a day and burning out."
- "My CTO said something in a 1:1 that changed everything."
- "It was 2 AM on a Saturday when I realized our entire architecture was wrong."

**Career:**
- "The email that changed my career was from someone I'd never met."
- "I was sitting in my car in the parking lot when I decided to quit."
- "They rejected me 7 times. Then I rewrote one line on my resume."

**Entrepreneurship:**
- "Nobody warned me about the loneliness of being a first-time founder."
- "The worst business decision of my life happened on an ordinary Tuesday."
- "Last month, something happened with my biggest client that I need to share."

## Category 5: Relatable Pain

These hooks name a frustration your audience already feels but hasn't articulated. When someone sees their own struggle described precisely, they feel understood and keep reading.

### Templates

1. "You spend [time] on [activity]. You get [disappointing result]."
2. "Everyone's talking about [trend]. Nobody's talking about [real issue]."
3. "Your [thing] isn't failing because of [obvious reason]."
4. "[Achievement] means nothing if [uncomfortable truth]."
5. "You do everything [authority] says. It still doesn't [work/convert/grow]."
6. "Tired of [common frustration]? It's not you. It's [the real problem]."
7. "That feeling when you [common professional frustration]? There's a name for it."
8. "You know what nobody tells you about [desirable thing]? [Hidden downside]."
9. "You've read [number] [books/articles/posts] on [topic] and you're still [stuck]."
10. "The [industry] is obsessed with [shiny thing] while ignoring [important thing]."
11. "Another [week/month/quarter] of [disappointing metric]. Same strategy. Why?"
12. "You work [many] hours. Your [peer/competitor] works [fewer]. They're outperforming you."
13. "If [popular advice] actually worked, everyone would be [desired outcome] by now."
14. "You don't have a [common diagnosis] problem. You have a [real diagnosis] problem."
15. "The gap between where you are and where you want to be isn't [effort]. It's [real answer]."

### Filled Examples

**Tech:**
- "You spend 3 hours in code review. You catch zero critical bugs."
- "Everyone's talking about AI. Nobody's talking about the engineers it's burning out."
- "Your app isn't failing because of the tech stack."

**Career:**
- "You do everything your career coach says. It still doesn't land interviews."
- "You've read 30 books on leadership and you're still struggling with your team."
- "You work 60 hours. Your colleague works 35. They got the promotion. Why?"

**Entrepreneurship:**
- "Another month of posting content. Same 200 impressions. Same 0 leads."
- "The startup world is obsessed with funding while ignoring profitability."
- "If 'just provide value' actually worked, every expert would be rich by now."

## Category 6: Pattern Interrupt

These hooks break the reader's scrolling pattern by being unexpected, self-aware, or meta. They work by violating LinkedIn norms, which creates surprise.

### Templates

1. "DELETE this post if you disagree."
2. "This will make [group] angry."
3. "I'm about to lose followers for this."
4. "Unpopular opinion that isn't actually unpopular:"
5. "Read this twice. Then read it again."
6. "This post has no hook. Just a truth you need to hear."
7. "I wasn't going to post this. Then I realized [reason]."
8. "Don't read this if you're [comfortable with status quo]."
9. "I've been thinking about [topic] for [time] and I'm ready to say it out loud."
10. "This is the post I would've needed [timeframe] ago."
11. "Hot take incoming. Unfollow button is top right."
12. "I'm going to get hate for this. But someone needs to say it."
13. "The most important [topic] post you'll read today (and you'll hate it)."
14. "I wrote this 3 times. Deleted it twice. Here's why I'm finally posting it."
15. "Stop scrolling. This one's for you."
16. "No framework. No tips. Just something I've been carrying."

### Filled Examples

**Tech:**
- "This will make senior engineers angry."
- "I wasn't going to post this. Then my junior dev said the same thing."
- "I've been thinking about technical debt for 10 years and I'm ready to say it out loud."

**Career:**
- "Don't read this if you're comfortable being underpaid."
- "I wrote this resignation post 3 times. Deleted it twice. Here's why I'm finally posting it."
- "Stop scrolling. This one's for every mid-career professional who feels stuck."

**Entrepreneurship:**
- "I'm about to lose followers for this. But every founder needs to hear it."
- "This is the post I would've needed 5 years ago before starting my business."
- "Hot take incoming. Unfollow button is top right. Still here? Good."

| List Post | Number Drop | Curiosity Gap |
| Before/After | Number Drop | Story Opener |
| Framework Reveal | Curiosity Gap | Number Drop |
| Hot Take | Bold Claim | Pattern Interrupt |
| Myth Buster | Bold Claim | Relatable Pain |
| Behind The Scenes | Story Opener | Number Drop |
| Data Drop | Number Drop | Curiosity Gap |
| Lessons From Failure | Story Opener | Pattern Interrupt |
| Quick Win | Relatable Pain | Number Drop |
| Comparison | Bold Claim | Curiosity Gap |
| Prediction | Bold Claim | Number Drop |
| Unpopular Opinion | Pattern Interrupt | Bold Claim |
| Case Study | Number Drop | Curiosity Gap |
| "This You?" | Relatable Pain | Pattern Interrupt |
| Thread Launcher | Curiosity Gap | Number Drop |

### Hook Length Guidelines

- **Ideal:** 8-15 words for the first line
- **Maximum:** 20 words (anything longer loses the scroll-stopping effect)
- **Format:** One line. Period. (Let it breathe with a line break after)
- **Avoid:** Questions as hooks (they perform 30% worse than statements on LinkedIn)
- **Exception:** Rhetorical questions that are really statements ("You know what nobody tells you about X?")

### Hook Testing Method

Before posting, test your hook against these filters:

1. **The Screenshot Test:** Would someone screenshot just the hook and share it?
2. **The "So What" Test:** Does the hook answer "why should I care?" in under 2 seconds?
3. **The Specificity Test:** Does the hook contain at least one specific detail (number, name, timeframe)?
4. **The Emotion Test:** Does the hook trigger curiosity, recognition, surprise, or disagreement?
5. **The Scroll-Stop Test:** Read it as if you were scrolling through 50 posts at speed. Would you stop?

If the hook passes 4 out of 5, post it. If it passes 3, revise. Below 3, start over.

## Advanced Hook Techniques

### The Double Hook

Use two short lines instead of one. The first creates tension, the second deepens it.

**Pattern:**
```
[Short shocking statement.]
[Follow-up that makes it personal or specific.]
```

**Examples:**
- "I got fired last Tuesday. / Best thing that ever happened."
- "$0 in revenue. 50,000 followers. / Vanity metrics almost killed my business."
- "My mentor was wrong. / About the one thing I trusted most."

### The Anti-Hook

Deliberately break hook conventions to stand out. Works best when your audience is saturated with typical hooks.

**Pattern:**
```
[Honest, unpolished statement that feels raw.]
```

**Examples:**
- "I don't have a framework for this. Just a hard truth."
- "No '7 tips.' No 'here's what I learned.' Just this:"
- "I have nothing to sell you. I just need to say this."

### The Callback Hook

Reference a previous post or common LinkedIn behavior to create meta-engagement.

**Pattern:**
```
[Reference to shared LinkedIn experience.]
```

**Examples:**
- "Last week I posted [topic] and 47 people DM'd me the same question."
- "Everyone shared my post about [topic]. Nobody did the thing I suggested."
- "You saved my last post. Did you actually use it? Be honest."

## Hook Bank by Industry Vertical

### For B2B SaaS
- "Your customers don't churn because of your product. They churn because of your onboarding."
- "We A/B tested [number] pricing pages. The winner broke every 'best practice.'"
- "ARR is a vanity metric. Here's what actually predicts survival."

### For Recruiting/HR
- "The best candidate in your pipeline right now? They're about to accept another offer."
- "I've reviewed [number] job descriptions this month. [Number] were actively repelling top talent."
- "Exit interviews are useless. Here's how to find out why people actually leave."

### For Marketing
- "Your brand voice document is 40 pages. Your audience can't describe you in one sentence."
- "I cut our content calendar by 70%. Traffic went up."
- "The campaign that generated $[X]M started as a rejected idea in a brainstorm."

### For Leadership/Management
- "Your team isn't disengaged. They're under-informed."
- "The meeting you could cancel today would give your team back [number] hours this quarter."
- "I asked my team for anonymous feedback. I wasn't ready for what they said."

### For Personal Branding/Career
- "Your dream company already looked at your LinkedIn profile. And passed."
- "I make [$X] more than I did 2 years ago. I changed one thing."
- "The skill that made you great as an IC is destroying you as a manager."
