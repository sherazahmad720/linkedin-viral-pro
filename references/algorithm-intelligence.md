# LinkedIn Algorithm Intelligence (2024-2026)

> Deep breakdown of how the LinkedIn algorithm evaluates, distributes, and amplifies content. Updated for the 2025-2026 algorithm shift toward "knowledge and expertise" content.

---

## 1. Signal Hierarchy

LinkedIn's algorithm ranks engagement signals by weight. Not all interactions are equal.

### Signal Rankings (Highest to Lowest Impact)

| Rank | Signal | Weight Multiplier | Why It Matters |
|------|--------|-------------------|----------------|
| 1 | **Dwell Time** | 5x | Time spent reading your post is the #1 signal. A user who reads for 15+ seconds sends a massive quality indicator, even without interacting. |
| 2 | **Meaningful Comments** | 4x | Comments of 5+ words, especially from outside your immediate network, signal value. The algorithm distinguishes between "Great post!" and substantive responses. |
| 3 | **Comment Replies (Conversations)** | 3.5x | When a comment turns into a back-and-forth thread (2+ replies), LinkedIn treats this as high-quality engagement. Each reply in a thread amplifies reach. |
| 4 | **Shares/Reposts** | 3x | Reposts (not shares with commentary) signal "this is worth passing along." However, LinkedIn has slightly devalued shares vs. 2023 to combat engagement bait. |
| 5 | **Saves/Bookmarks** | 2.5x | A relatively new but powerful signal (elevated in 2024). Saves indicate reference-quality content, which LinkedIn rewards heavily. |
| 6 | **Reactions** | 1.5x | Likes and reactions are the weakest active signal. "Celebrate" and "Insightful" reactions carry marginally more weight than a basic "Like." |
| 7 | **Profile Clicks** | 1x | When someone clicks your profile after seeing your post, it signals curiosity but doesn't directly boost distribution. |
| 8 | **External Link Clicks** | 0.5x | Clicks on external links are counted as engagement but are actively penalized in reach (see Penalty Triggers below). |

### How to Optimize for Each Signal

**Dwell Time:**
- Write longer posts (1,000-1,800 characters perform best for dwell time)
- Use line breaks and white space to control reading pace
- Include a narrative arc -- stories force sequential reading
- Use document/carousel posts (swipe-through time counts as dwell time)
- Avoid giving the punchline in the first two lines

**Meaningful Comments:**
- End every post with a specific question (not generic "thoughts?")
- Binary questions ("Team A or Team B?") have 2x the comment rate of open-ended
- Controversial opinions naturally generate longer comments
- Reply to every comment within the first 2 hours to keep threads growing

**Saves:**
- Posts with frameworks, checklists, step-by-step guides get saved
- Explicitly say "save this for later" (it actually increases save rate by 30-40%)
- Lists and numbered content have the highest save rate
- Document/carousel posts are saved at 3x the rate of text posts

## 2. First-Hour Dynamics

The first 60 minutes after posting determine 80%+ of your post's total reach. Here's the exact mechanism.

### The Distribution Funnel

**Phase 1: Quality Check (0-15 minutes)**
- LinkedIn's AI scans your post for: spam triggers, engagement bait phrases, external links, low-quality patterns
- Your post is shown to a "seed audience" of approximately 5-10% of your first-degree connections
- This seed group is selected based on: topic affinity, past engagement with your content, and current online status

**Phase 2: Testing Pool (15-60 minutes)**
- LinkedIn measures engagement velocity: what percentage of the seed audience engaged, and how
- Key threshold: If 3-5% of the seed audience engages meaningfully within 30 minutes, the algorithm expands distribution
- Engagement velocity = (Engagements / Impressions) in the first 60 minutes
- Target velocity: >2% engagement rate in the first hour signals a high-quality post

**Phase 3: Network Expansion (1-4 hours)**
- If Phase 2 metrics are strong, your post is shown to:
  - Remaining first-degree connections (50-80% of your network)
  - Second-degree connections (connections of people who engaged)
  - Topic followers (people who follow hashtags you used)
- This is where posts either plateau or go viral

**Phase 4: Extended Distribution (4-48 hours)**
- High-performing posts enter a secondary distribution cycle
- Shown to: broader topic communities, "suggested" feeds, notification digests
- Posts can receive 30-60% of total impressions during this phase
- Very high performers continue getting distribution for 3-7 days

**Phase 5: Long Tail (48 hours - 2 weeks)**
- Posts that continue receiving engagement (especially new comments) can be re-surfaced
- LinkedIn's "Top Posts" feature can surface week-old content to new audiences
- Comments on old posts can restart limited distribution

### Actionable First-Hour Strategy

1. **Post when your core audience is online** (see Best Posting Times below)
2. **Do NOT edit your post within the first hour** (editing can reset distribution)
3. **Reply to every comment immediately** during the first hour (keeps velocity up)
4. **Engage on other people's posts** 20-30 minutes before AND after your post goes live (signals active participation to the algorithm)
5. **Have 3-5 trusted colleagues engage authentically** within the first 15 minutes (not pods -- genuine engagement from people who actually read the content)
6. **Put external links in the first comment**, not the post body (avoids the link penalty during the critical first hour)

## 3. Format Performance Ranking

Based on aggregate data from 2024-2026, here's how each content format performs. Rankings shift gradually -- this reflects the current state.

### Current Format Rankings (2025-2026)

| Rank | Format | Avg. Reach Multiplier | Why |
|------|--------|----------------------|-----|
| 1 | **Document/Carousel Posts** | 2.5-4x | LinkedIn actively promotes this format. High dwell time (swipe-through), high save rate. The algorithm shows carousels to non-connections more than any other format. |
| 2 | **Text-Only Posts** | 1.5-3x | Highest comment rate. LinkedIn's 2024-2025 shift toward "expertise" content favors well-written text. Low production cost means higher posting frequency. |
| 3 | **Text + Single Image** | 1.2-2.5x | Images stop the scroll but don't drive as many comments as pure text. Best when the image adds information (charts, diagrams) rather than decoration. |
| 4 | **Native Video** | 1-2x | LinkedIn is pushing short-form video (under 90 seconds) in 2025-2026 but hasn't cracked the engagement code. Watch time is the key metric. Long videos (3+ min) see significant drop-off. |
| 5 | **Polls** | 0.8-1.5x | Nerfed significantly in 2024 after overuse. Still get decent reach if the poll question is genuinely interesting. The algorithm now penalizes low-effort polls. |
| 6 | **Articles/Newsletters** | 0.5-1x | LinkedIn newsletters get sent to subscriber inboxes (powerful for existing audiences). But articles/newsletters get lower feed distribution than posts. Best as a secondary format. |
| 7 | **External Link Posts** | 0.3-0.7x | The worst-performing format by far. LinkedIn actively suppresses posts with external links to keep users on-platform. A link in the post body can reduce reach by 30-50%. |

### Format Selection Guide

| Content Type | Best Format |
|-------------|-------------|
| Frameworks, guides, step-by-step content | Document/Carousel |
| Personal stories, opinions, hot takes | Text-only |
| Data-driven insights | Text + Image (chart/graph) |
| Tutorials, demos, "how I did it" | Short native video (60-90 sec) |
| Quick engagement, audience research | Poll (use sparingly, max 1/week) |
| Deep dives, thought leadership archives | Newsletter article |
| Anything with an external link | Text post + link in first comment |

### Carousel/Document Best Practices

- **Optimal slides:** 8-12 slides (sweet spot for engagement + completion rate)
- **First slide:** Must be a hook/title that works as a scroll-stopper
- **Last slide:** CTA + profile handle for shareability
- **Format:** PDF upload (not PowerPoint -- PDF renders more reliably)
- **Size:** 1080x1350 pixels (4:5 ratio, optimized for mobile)
- **Text per slide:** 30-50 words maximum (readability on mobile)
- **Design:** High contrast, large fonts (16pt+), consistent branding

## 4. Optimal Post Length

Character count affects both dwell time and readability. The sweet spot depends on content type.

### Length Tiers

**Short Posts (300-600 characters / ~50-100 words)**
- Best for: Quick wins, hot takes, bold claims, pattern interrupts
- Strengths: Easy to read, high comment rate (low barrier to engagement)
- Weakness: Lower dwell time, can feel shallow
- Use when: You have ONE strong point and don't need to elaborate
- Post frequency: 1-2x per week max (too many short posts signal low effort)

**Medium Posts (800-1,200 characters / ~130-200 words)**
- Best for: Lists, comparisons, myth busters, unpopular opinions
- Strengths: Balanced dwell time and readability, triggers "see more" click (which is a positive signal)
- Weakness: Can feel incomplete if the topic demands depth
- Use when: You have 3-5 points to make and each point is concise
- Post frequency: This should be your most common length (3-4x per week)

**Long Posts (1,500-2,000 characters / ~250-330 words)**
- Best for: Story arcs, case studies, framework reveals, lessons from failure
- Strengths: Maximum dwell time, feels like premium content, high save rate
- Weakness: Higher drop-off if the writing isn't compelling line by line
- Use when: You have a full narrative arc or comprehensive framework
- Post frequency: 1-2x per week (reserve for your best content)

**Very Long Posts (2,500-3,000 characters / LinkedIn's ~3,000 character limit)**
- Best for: Only use when every word earns its place -- deep case studies, series launchers
- Strengths: Extremely high dwell time if the reader stays engaged
- Weakness: 40-60% of readers won't finish; requires masterful writing
- Use when: Almost never. If you need this much space, consider a carousel instead.
- Post frequency: 1-2x per month maximum

### The "See More" Click

Posts over ~210 characters (approximately 3 lines on mobile) get truncated with a "see more" button. **This is a feature, not a bug.**

- The "see more" click counts as an engagement signal
- Your first 2-3 lines (the "above the fold" content) must compel the click
- Put your hook in line 1, a line break, then a second line that deepens the hook
- Never put your main point above the fold -- the reader should NEED to click "see more"

### Formatting Rules for Readability

1. **One sentence per line** (maximum two short sentences per line)
2. **Line break between every thought** (white space is your friend)
3. **No paragraphs longer than 3 lines** on mobile (check mobile preview)
4. **Use special characters sparingly** for structure: arrows, bullet points, dashes
5. **Avoid emojis as bullet points** (LinkedIn's 2025 algorithm slightly penalizes emoji-heavy posts as engagement bait)
6. **Bold text** can be created using Unicode characters but use sparingly -- overuse looks spammy

## 5. Hashtag Strategy

Hashtags on LinkedIn function as topic categorization for the algorithm, not as discovery mechanisms like Instagram.

### Current Best Practices (2025-2026)

**Quantity:** 3-5 hashtags per post
- Fewer than 3: Misses categorization opportunity
- More than 5: Diminishing returns; can look spammy
- The algorithm reportedly ignores hashtags beyond the 5th

**Composition Mix:**
| Type | Followers | Example | Purpose |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|
| Broad (1-2) | 500K-10M+ | #leadership, #marketing, #technology | Access to large topic pools |
| Medium (1-2) | 50K-500K | #contentmarketing, #startuplife, #productmanagement | Targeted reach |
| Niche (1) | 5K-50K | #b2bsaas, #techrecruiting, #founderstory | Highly relevant audience, lower competition |

**Placement:**
- Place hashtags at the END of your post (after the CTA)
- Separate from the body text with a line break
- Do NOT embed hashtags within sentences (looks amateurish and interrupts reading flow)

**Hashtags to Avoid:**
- Banned/spam hashtags (LinkedIn doesn't publish a list, but avoid: #follow, #like, #comment, #viral)
- Overly generic (#business, #life, #success -- too broad to help)
- Branded hashtags with no followers (unless you're deliberately building one)
- Trending hashtags unrelated to your content (algorithm sees this as manipulation)

### High-Performing Hashtag Sets by Niche

**Tech/Engineering:**
#softwaredevelopment #techleadership #engineering + 1-2 niche

**Career/Professional Growth:**
#careeradvice #professionaldevelopment #jobsearch + 1-2 niche

**Entrepreneurship/Startups:**
#entrepreneurship #startups #founderlife + 1-2 niche

**Marketing:**
#digitalmarketing #contentmarketing #branding + 1-2 niche

**Leadership/Management:**

## 6. Engagement Quality Signals

Not all engagement is equal. LinkedIn's algorithm has become increasingly sophisticated at distinguishing genuine engagement from manufactured signals.

### Comment Quality Hierarchy

| Comment Type | Algorithm Value | Examples |
|-------------|----------------|----------|
| **Substantive (5+ words with original thought)** | Very High | "I tried this approach with my team and found that step 3 works best when combined with weekly check-ins." |
| **Question-based** | High | "How would this work for a remote team of 50+?" |
| **Short agreement with context** | Medium | "This matches my experience at [Company]." |
| **Tagging (with context)** | Medium | "@Name this is the framework we discussed" |
| **Generic short reactions** | Low | "Love this!" "So true!" "Great post!" |
| **Emoji-only** | Very Low | Fire emojis, clap emojis, etc. |
| **Engagement pod comments** | Negative | Detected patterns result in reach suppression |

### Reply Speed Impact

Your response time to comments directly affects distribution:

| Response Time | Impact on Reach |
|--------------|----------------|
| Within 15 minutes | Maximum boost -- signals active conversation |
| 15-60 minutes | Strong boost -- still within the critical window |
| 1-4 hours | Moderate boost -- keeps the thread alive |
| 4-24 hours | Minimal boost -- conversation has gone cold |
| 24+ hours | No boost -- but still worth replying for relationship building |

### Engagement Reciprocity

LinkedIn tracks engagement reciprocity. If someone consistently engages with your content and you never engage with theirs, the algorithm reduces how often your content appears in their feed. The algorithm rewards two-way relationships.

**Actionable strategy:**
- Maintain a "top 20" list of people whose content you consistently engage with
- Spend 15-20 minutes engaging on others' posts BEFORE you publish your own
- Leave substantive comments (8+ words) on posts from people in your target audience
- This primes the algorithm to surface your next post to their network

## 7. Profile Strength Multiplier

Your profile quality acts as a multiplier on all content distribution. Two people posting identical content will get different reach based on profile signals.

### Social Selling Index (SSI)

LinkedIn's SSI score (0-100) is based on four pillars:

| Pillar | Weight | How to Optimize |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| **Establish your professional brand** | 25 points | Complete profile, long-form content publishing, endorsements received |
| **Find the right people** | 25 points | Advanced search usage, profile views of decision-makers |
| **Engage with insights** | 25 points | Content sharing, commenting frequency, post engagement rate |
| **Build relationships** | 25 points | Connection acceptance rate, InMail response rate, network growth |

**Check your SSI:** linkedin.com/sales/ssi

**Target SSI by goal:**
- Content creation focus: 70+
- Thought leadership: 80+
- Social selling: 85+

### Profile Completeness Factors

| Factor | Impact on Content Reach |
|--------|------------------------|
| Professional headshot | +10-15% (posts from profiles with photos get significantly more engagement) |
| Custom headline (not job title) | +5-10% (descriptive headlines signal content creator) |
| About section (filled, keyword-rich) | +5% (helps algorithm categorize your content) |
| Featured section (populated) | Marginal direct impact, but increases profile visit-to-follow conversion |
| Experience section (complete) | Marginal -- matters more for search than feed distribution |
| Creator Mode ON | +10-20% (LinkedIn gives creator mode profiles more feed visibility) |
| Custom URL | No direct algorithm impact, but signals professionalism |

### Connection Quality vs. Quantity

- **Quality connections** (people in your industry who actively engage) > thousands of random connections
- The algorithm weights engagement from people the reader is also connected to
- Mutual connections who engage create a "social proof" signal
- Having connections who are themselves high-engagement users amplifies your reach

## 8. Posting Frequency

### The Diminishing Returns Curve

| Posts per Week | Average Reach per Post | Total Weekly Reach |
|---------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| 1-2 | Baseline (100%) | 1-2x |
| 3-4 | 90-95% of baseline | 2.7-3.8x |
| 5 (daily weekdays) | 80-90% of baseline | 4-4.5x |
| 6-7 | 60-70% of baseline | 3.6-4.9x |
| 8-10 | 40-50% of baseline | 3.2-5x |
| 10+ | 30-40% of baseline | Diminishing heavily |

### Optimal Frequency Strategy

**Sweet spot: 4-5 posts per week (weekdays)**

- The algorithm slightly penalizes your second post if you post twice in the same day
- Minimum 8-hour gap between posts to avoid cannibalization
- If you must post twice in a day, use different formats (e.g., text post + carousel)

**Recommended weekly schedule:**
- Monday: Skip (lowest LinkedIn engagement day) or post lighter content
- Tuesday: High-effort post (story arc, case study, framework reveal)
- Wednesday: Medium-effort post (list, quick win, comparison)
- Thursday: High-effort post (hot take, contrarian, data drop)
- Friday: Lighter post (behind the scenes, unpopular opinion, relatable content)
- Saturday-Sunday: Optional. Weekend reach is 30-50% lower but competition is also lower.

### Consistency Over Volume

The algorithm rewards consistent posting patterns:
- Posting 3x/week every week > posting 7x one week and 0 the next
- LinkedIn tracks your posting velocity and adjusts expected engagement accordingly
- A sudden drop in frequency can temporarily reduce your baseline reach
- A sudden increase doesn't immediately boost reach -- the algorithm needs 2-3 weeks to recalibrate

## 10. Content Type Trends 2025-2026

### What LinkedIn Is Currently Promoting

**Actively Boosted:**

1. **Expertise and knowledge-sharing content**
   - LinkedIn's 2024-2025 algorithm update explicitly rewards posts that demonstrate professional expertise
   - The algorithm now uses AI to assess "knowledge quality" -- generic advice gets less distribution than specific, experience-based insights
   - Posts that reference specific numbers, case studies, or frameworks are classified as higher quality

2. **Personal stories with professional relevance**
   - Vulnerability + insight continues to be the highest-engagement combination
   - The algorithm distinguishes between "genuine personal story" and "life update" -- the former gets boosted, the latter doesn't
   - Key: The story must have a professional takeaway, not just be a personal diary entry

3. **Document/carousel posts**
   - LinkedIn has invested heavily in the document viewing experience
   - Carousel completion rate is a strong signal (similar to video watch time)
   - Educational carousels see 2-3x more reach than equivalent text posts

4. **Collaborative articles and community engagement**
   - Contributing to collaborative articles builds "top voice" badges
   - These badges act as a reach multiplier on all your content
   - LinkedIn is expanding this program in 2025-2026

5. **Short-form video (under 90 seconds)**
   - LinkedIn's answer to TikTok/Reels is gaining traction
   - Early adopters of LinkedIn video are seeing inflated reach (the "new format boost")
   - Vertical video (9:16) is being prioritized in mobile feeds

**Declining/Penalized:**

1. **Engagement bait** -- "Like if you agree, comment if you disagree" style posts see 30-50% reach reduction
2. **Pod-driven engagement** -- LinkedIn's detection has improved dramatically; pod activity can result in shadow-banning
3. **Outrage content without substance** -- Pure negativity without insight is being down-ranked
4. **Selfie-with-caption posts** -- Low-effort personal updates are being deprioritized in favor of knowledge content
5. **Hashtag stuffing** -- More than 5-7 hashtags now triggers a spam signal
6. **Chain posts/copy-paste content** -- "Copy this to your profile" style posts are penalized
7. **Excessive self-promotion** -- Posts that are primarily about selling a product/service get reduced distribution

### Emerging Opportunities (2026)

- **AI-powered content** is everywhere -- posts that feel distinctly human (imperfect, specific, experiential) will stand out
- **Video podcasting clips** are gaining traction as a LinkedIn content format
- **Interactive content** (polls with follow-up analysis posts) creates multi-day engagement loops
- **"Build in public" narratives** with real numbers continue to be underleveraged and high-performing
- **Niche expertise** beats broad advice -- the algorithm is getting better at matching specialists with their exact audience

## 11. Penalty Triggers

Actions and patterns that actively reduce your content's distribution.

### High-Impact Penalties

| Trigger | Reach Reduction | Duration |
|---------|----------------|----------|
| **External links in post body** | -30-50% | Per-post |
| **Engagement bait phrases** | -20-40% | Per-post, can compound |
| **Editing post within first hour** | -10-30% (unconfirmed but widely observed) | Per-post |
| **Detected pod activity** | -50-80% (can result in shadow ban) | Weeks to months |
| **Posting 2+ times in <4 hours** | -20-30% on the second post | Per-post |
| **Tagging 5+ people who don't engage** | -10-20% | Per-post |
| **Deleted and reposted content** | Variable -- algorithm may recognize duplicate | Per-post |

### External Link Penalty -- The Full Picture

The link penalty is the most impactful and most misunderstood:

- **In-post links** reduce reach by 30-50% because LinkedIn wants to keep users on-platform
- **First-comment links** avoid most of the penalty but still see a ~5-10% reduction
- **No link at all** is the best-performing option
- **Workaround:** Say "link in comments" or "link in my featured section" and put the URL in the first comment or your profile
- **Exception:** LinkedIn.com links (to other LinkedIn posts, profiles, or newsletter articles) see minimal penalty

### Engagement Bait Phrases to Avoid

LinkedIn's algorithm can detect these patterns:

- "Like if you agree"
- "Comment [word] for [thing]" (this now gets flagged; use sparingly)
- "Share this with your network"
- "Tag someone who needs this"
- "Can we get 1,000 likes?"
- "I bet no one will share this"
- Excessive use of "Follow me for more"

**Safe alternatives:**
- "What's your take on this?" (genuine question, not bait)
- "Save this for when you need it" (not flagged as bait in current algorithm)
- "Which point resonated most?" (invites substantive response)

### Post Editing Risks

- LinkedIn has stated editing is fine, but widespread testing shows a reach dip when editing within the first 60 minutes
- **If you find a typo:** Wait until the post is past its first-hour window, then edit
- **If there's a factual error:** Edit immediately -- accuracy matters more than algorithm timing
- **Adding content to an existing post:** Generally safe after 4+ hours but won't re-trigger distribution

### Shadow Banning Indicators

LinkedIn doesn't officially shadow ban, but content suppression happens:
- Your impressions suddenly drop to 10-20% of normal across ALL posts
- Your posts stop appearing in hashtag feeds
- Comments on your posts come only from direct notifications, not feed visibility

**Recovery:**
- Stop posting for 3-5 days
- Remove any automated tools connected to your account
- Return with genuine, high-quality content
- Engage authentically (not through pods) for 1-2 weeks before returning to regular posting

## 12. The Comment Game

Strategic commenting is the single most underrated growth lever on LinkedIn. Done correctly, it can grow your audience faster than posting.

### The Commenting Strategy Framework

**Pre-Post Priming (30 minutes before your post):**
1. Open LinkedIn 30 minutes before you plan to post
2. Leave 5-10 substantive comments on posts from your target audience / ideal connections
3. Each comment should be 2-3 sentences minimum, adding genuine value
4. This does two things: warms up the algorithm to your activity AND puts you top-of-mind for people who will see your post in their feed shortly

**Post-Publish Engagement (30-60 minutes after your post):**
1. Continue commenting on others' posts (5-10 more comments)
2. Reply to EVERY comment on your own post within the first hour
3. Ask follow-up questions to commenters (turns comments into conversations)
4. This maintains your "active session" signal, which LinkedIn rewards with extended distribution

### Comment Quality Guidelines

**A great comment:**
- Adds a perspective the original poster didn't cover
- Shares a personal experience that validates or challenges the point
- Asks a specific, thoughtful question
- Is 2-5 sentences (long enough to signal thought, short enough to be read)
- Starts with the insight, not with flattery

**Bad comment examples (avoid):**
- "Great post!" (zero value)
- "Couldn't agree more!" (zero value)
- "Following for updates" (zero value)
- A paragraph that's really about you, not the post (self-promotional)
- A comment that just repeats what the post said (adds nothing)

**Good comment template:**
"[Your take on the main point]. I've seen this play out in [your context]. The part I'd add is [unique perspective]. Question: [specific follow-up]."

### The Reciprocity Engine

When you leave thoughtful comments consistently, three things happen:

1. **The poster notices you** and is more likely to engage with your content
2. **The poster's audience sees your comment** (especially if it gets likes or replies), driving profile visits
3. **The algorithm learns your engagement pattern** and shows your content to people you frequently engage with

### Advanced: Strategic Commenting for Growth

**Identify 10-20 accounts that:**
- Post to your target audience
- Have larger followings than you (but not 10x larger -- they won't notice you)
- Post regularly (3-5x per week)
- Actually respond to comments

**Then:**
- Be among the first 5 commenters on their posts (early comments get more visibility)
- Comment with genuine insight (not sycophancy)
- Do this consistently for 30 days
- Expected result: 20-50% increase in your own post engagement, plus follower growth from profile visits

### Comment-to-Content Pipeline

Your best comments often become great posts:
- When a comment gets 10+ likes, expand it into a full post
- When you find yourself writing a 5+ sentence comment, save it and post it as standalone content
- When a comment sparks a debate thread, write a post addressing the debate
